Resume Development
Weeks 3‚Äì4 ¬∑ Mar 5 ‚Äì Mar 23

Why This Matters

Your resume is the document that gets you interviews. Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) ‚Äî software that scans and filters resumes before a human ever sees them. If your resume isn't ATS-friendly, it gets rejected automatically, no matter how qualified you are. The instructions below will help you create a resume that passes ATS filters AND looks professional to human readers.

ATS-Friendly Resume Rules

Follow these rules so your resume is not rejected by automated systems:

‚úì
Use a simple, single-column layout. ATS cannot read multi-column formats, tables, or text boxes. Stick to a straightforward top-to-bottom layout.
‚úì
Use standard section headings. Use exactly these words: "Professional Summary," "Education," "Experience," "Skills." Creative headings like "My Journey" or "Toolbox" confuse ATS.
‚úì
Use standard fonts. Calibri, Arial, Georgia, or Times New Roman in 10‚Äì12pt. No decorative fonts.
‚úì
Use keywords from the job posting. If the posting says "data analysis," use those exact words ‚Äî not "number crunching." Mirror the language of the job description.
‚úó
No images, icons, logos, or graphics. ATS can't read them. No headshot, no skill bar charts, no decorative lines.
‚úó
No headers or footers. ATS often ignores text in header/footer areas. Put your contact info in the main body of the document.
‚úó
No fancy Canva resume templates. They look great but almost always use columns, text boxes, or graphics that break ATS parsing. Use Google Docs or Microsoft Word templates instead.

Step-by-Step

ü§ñ AI Prompt Guide ‚Äî Tailor Your Resume Like a Pro

Use these prompts with any free AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or Copilot). They are designed as a chain ‚Äî each step builds on the previous one. Copy and paste them in order. Important: AI gives you a starting draft. You must review, fact-check, and rewrite everything in your own voice before using it.

Prompt 1
Analyze the Job Posting
I'm a student building my first professional resume. I found a job/internship posting I'm interested in. Please analyze it step by step:

1. List the top 5 hard skills the employer is looking for.
2. List the top 3 soft skills the employer is looking for.
3. Identify any specific keywords or phrases that appear repeatedly.
4. Identify the experience level expected (entry-level, intern, etc.).
5. Summarize in 2 sentences what this employer values most in a candidate.

Here is the job posting:
[PASTE THE FULL JOB POSTING TEXT HERE]
Prompt 2
Match Your Experience to the Job
Now here is my background. Please think step by step about how my experience connects to this job, even if the connection isn't obvious:

- I'm currently studying: [YOUR PROGRAM, e.g., "Pre-Calculus and General Studies at a community college"]
- Relevant coursework: [LIST COURSES, e.g., "Pre-Calculus, Statistics, Intro to Business"]
- Work/volunteer experience: [LIST ANY JOBS, VOLUNTEERING, TUTORING, e.g., "Part-time cashier at Target for 1 year, math tutor for classmates"]
- Skills I have: [LIST YOUR SKILLS, e.g., "Excel, Google Sheets, problem solving, customer service"]
- Projects or achievements: [ANY NOTABLE ITEMS, e.g., "Built a data visualization project in my math class"]

Based on the job posting analysis from Prompt 1, please:
1. Identify which of my experiences are most relevant and explain why.
2. Suggest how to describe each experience using keywords from the job posting.
3. Identify any skill gaps and suggest ways I could address them.
4. Write 3 bullet points for each experience using strong action verbs and the employer's language.
Prompt 3
Generate an ATS-Optimized Resume Draft
Based on everything we've discussed, please write a complete one-page resume for me. Follow these rules exactly:

FORMAT RULES (ATS-compliant):
- Single-column layout, no tables or text boxes
- Use only these section headings in this order: Professional Summary, Education, Experience, Skills
- Standard font (Calibri or Arial), 10-12pt
- No icons, images, or graphics of any kind
- No header or footer ‚Äî put contact info at the top of the body

CONTENT RULES:
- Professional Summary: 2-3 sentences using keywords from the job posting
- Education: Include school, program, expected graduation, GPA if 3.0+, relevant coursework
- Experience: Use action verbs, quantify results where possible, mirror the job posting language
- Skills: Combine hard and soft skills, prioritizing those mentioned in the job posting

My contact information:
- Name: [YOUR NAME]
- Email: [YOUR EMAIL]
- Phone: [YOUR PHONE]
- City, State: [YOUR CITY, STATE]
- LinkedIn: [YOUR LINKEDIN URL]

Write the complete resume now.
Prompt 4
Review & Improve
Please review the resume you just created and check it against these criteria:

1. ATS SCAN: Does every section use a standard heading? Are there any formatting elements that would confuse an ATS (tables, columns, graphics)?
2. KEYWORD MATCH: Compare the resume to the original job posting. What percentage of the key skills and requirements are reflected? What's missing?
3. IMPACT: Are bullet points specific and results-oriented, or vague and generic? Rewrite any weak bullets.
4. LENGTH: Is it truly one page at 10-12pt font with normal margins?
5. TONE: Does it sound professional but authentic ‚Äî like a real student wrote it, not a robot?

Please provide the improved final version with all fixes applied.

Math Artifact Creation
Weeks 5‚Äì6 ¬∑ Mar 23 ‚Äì Apr 6

Why This Matters

Employers and admissions committees want to see that you can apply math to real-world problems ‚Äî not just solve textbook exercises. Your math artifact demonstrates that you understand concepts deeply enough to connect them to a career context. This becomes a portfolio piece you can reference in interviews and applications.

What Is a Math Artifact?

A concise, visual showcase that demonstrates how a Pre-Calculus concept applies to a real-world scenario. It should be something you're proud to show a future employer or admissions officer. You may choose any Pre-Calculus topic covered in this course. Pick a topic you feel confident about and that connects to your career interests if possible.

Artifact Format Options

Choose ONE format for your artifact:

  • Option A ‚Äî Infographic: A single-page visual (use Canva, free) that explains the math concept, shows the real-world application, and includes at least one graph or calculation.
  • Option B ‚Äî Mini Case Study: A 1-page write-up (300‚Äì500 words) that presents a real scenario, applies the math, and interprets the results. Include at least one visual (graph, table, or chart).
  • Option C ‚Äî Data Visualization: Use a spreadsheet tool (Google Sheets, Excel) to model real data using your Pre-Calculus concept. Include the chart/graph and a short paragraph explaining what the data shows.

Why This Matters

An ePortfolio gives you a single link you can share with employers, transfer admissions, and scholarship committees. It tells your professional story in a way that a resume alone cannot. You will maintain and update this portfolio long after this class ends.

Choose Your Portfolio Platform

You may build your portfolio using ANY of the following options. Pick the one you're most comfortable with ‚Äî all are free and produce a professional result:

Option A ‚Äî HTML Template
Download the instructor-provided template. Edit in any text editor. No account needed. You own the file completely. Host it free on Netlify, GitHub Pages, or Tiiny Host.
Option B ‚Äî Google Sites
Drag-and-drop builder. Free with your Google account. Easy to update anytime. Professional URL available. Great if you prefer visual editing.
Option C ‚Äî Canva Website
Use Canva's free website templates. Design-focused with modern layouts. Publish directly from Canva. Good if you enjoy visual design.
Option D ‚Äî Other Platform
Wix, WordPress, Notion, or any other free portfolio platform is acceptable as long as it meets the requirements below and produces a shareable link.

Required Portfolio Sections

Regardless of which platform you choose, your portfolio must include all of these sections:

  • Header: Your name, professional tagline, and photo (optional)
  • About Me: A short professional bio (3‚Äì5 sentences)
  • Career Goal: Your target career direction and why
  • Math Artifact: Your artifact with title, image/embed, and reflection
  • Skills: Key skills relevant to your career direction
  • Links: LinkedIn profile URL (or note "Professional Bio available upon request" if you chose the Bio PDF path) and resume (PDF link)
  • Contact: Professional email
